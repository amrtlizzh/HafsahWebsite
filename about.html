Background

Mahallah Hafsa, one of the 9 female residential areas at IIUM, stands as one
of the earliest established mahallahs and has the capacity to house
approximately 856 students simultaneously. Our mahallah is conveniently
located in close proximity to various central facilities of IIUM, including the
IIUM Female Sport Centre and IIUM Health and Wellness Centre.
The name "Mahallah Hafsa" is derived from the name of the wife of the

Prophet Muhammad (SAW), Hafsa, who was the daughter of Umar ibn al-
Khattab, one of the Khulafa' ar-Rashidin (Rightly Guided Caliphs). Hafsa was
married to the Prophet Muhammad (SAW) following the death of her first
husband, Khunais, not long after the Battle of Badr.

Overview

Location: Opposite to Mahallah Aminah and next to Mahallah Asma.

Mahallah Hafsa comprises of 7 blocks where one block (PG Block) is
specifically for postgraduate student and six other blocks (Block C, D, E, F, G,
H) are for undergraduate students. Mahallah Hafsa has its own cafeteria.
